CONNECTIONS

COMPUTER ARTS WORKSHOP · by Fuzzy Binaires

CONNECTIONS is a 7-day intensive workshop program that focuses on the connecting power of creative computation. We are seeking collaboration and community through our shared practices of open coding, tinkering and making art.

During the week, a diverse group of participants will learn together and create prototypes.

Throughout the week, FUZZY BINAIRES will facilitate the learning of frameworks for creative computation and electronic arts, offering workshops ranging from DIY electronics to drawing, games, creative coding and prototyping. As part of the workshop activities, participants will be asked to present their own artistic practice and discuss possible connections with technology.

On the last day, participants can present their finished or ongoing projects, in the form of an experimental popup exhibition and thus invite an interested audience to their networks.

Statement

Mission

The mission of Fuzzy Binaires is to gently bridge the gap between art and computation. It is to create the connections necessary to explore how practices like computer programming, game development or decentralized collaborative sharing can become suitable means of artistic creation.

Understanding how an algorithm becomes a brush stroke, how sensors transform a space, or how procedurally-generated narratives drive a game's experience, is all part of the purpose of becoming a Fuzzy Binary: it is exploring the in-betweens and the end-points of the interface between humans and computers. Along the way, we will also learn about the ethical implications that technology carries with it, and how it is transforming society from an artistic perspective.

Becoming a Fuzzy Binaire is also about building a community of like-minded artists and technologist who enjoy art and coding, about promoting an open and positive environment, where ideas are encouraged and where learning is done by fostering curiosity and the enjoyment of the process. Finally, it is also about creating accessible opportunities and prioritizing the application of groups of people often marginalized in the tech industry.

Background thoughts

With the rise of technology-based frameworks for creative inquiry, the world has seen a very diverse, eclectic, and extremely interdisciplinary approach towards the creation of artwork. Ideas and practices that date back to the 1970s are now widespread among a community of art-making individuals, who are successfully merging and creating dialogues between their own practice by means of the power and language of computational devices.

For some artist, the idea of needing to learn the technicalities that computers entail might be daunting at first. It means learning a new language. It means understanding the very complex realm of the digital. It means crossing a bridge between the traditional and the future. And even for the experienced electronic artist, the umbrella of the computerized medium is so intricate and labyrinthian, that it might seem intimidating to stay up-to-date with the latest algorithms for anything...!!

Objectives

  • To introduce artists from different backgrounds to programming and into the craftsmanship involved in creative computing.
  • To facilitate the learning of the technical aspects, by providing hands-on practice with software programming and basic electronics, through a variety of workshops of well-known topics typically addressed in Electronic Arts.
  • To nurture the exchange of ideas and knowledge between creators from diverse practices and abilities, participants will have a chance to present a topic of their choice as part of the program agenda.
  • To Research and work on the importance of group dynamics geared towards enjoying and enhancing the learning experience.

Intended Audience

This workshop is aimed at students, artists, designers, makers, technologists, scientists, educators, and researchers who wish to expand their own practice and perception of the arts, while also enjoying a time of free-spirited techno-artistic production. Eligible participants may have never coded before, may be beginners who are curious about creative coding, or may also be experienced programmers who want to understand and bend their practice through the eyes of electronic arts and interactivity.

Activities

The workshops will take place from August/September XXXX, 2019, at Kreativquartier, Munich (Address).

  • Starting the week
    To start the week we will be playfully diving into the world of computers by using MineCraft to create logic gate systems, the building blocks of any digital computational devices.
    Later on, we will be transporting this into the realm of the physical, by building logic devices with actual electronic components.
  • Daily Morning Gatherings
    Morning group activities to energize our body, mind, spirit, but also an early space to share new ideas and thoughts. There will be freestyle Improv games and optional roundtables while we sip some coffee or tea.
  • Software Workshops
    We will be learning about digital frameworks for the creative manipulation of visuals and interactivity such as Processing, and Unity Game Engine. We will start with the basics, then go on to explore topics such as generative systems and sensor-based interaction.
  • Electronics Workshops
    Working with building analog and digital sensor/actuator systems, understanding prototyping platforms like Arduino, and testing out its integration with software.
  • Theory, Design
    Game Design, Electronic Arts, Computational Thinking, Machine Learning, Post-Human Reading Practices, Digital Culture, Digital Fabrication, among other workshops.
  • Presentations, Personal Project, Group Discussion
    Events to share the evolution of each of the participants' projects, with open discussion and critique. Approaching the end of the program, more time will be dedicated to working with the participants in the development of their own project.
  • Pop-Up Exhibition

    Final presentations of prototypes, experiments and works. Although this is encouraged and promoted, participants are totally free to opt out.

    • Setup exhibition throughout morning/early afternoon.
    • Afternoon Opening of exhibition.
    • Setup exhibition throughout morning/early afternoon.
    • Artist talk about their processes and week experience with the community.
    • Late Round-up and Feedback. An opportunity to internally discuss the "Connections" program.

Apply

  • Applicants need to submit:
    • Short bio: (1000-1500 characters).
    • Portfolio of work of their own practice (PDF or link to website). Some applicants might not have a portfolio yet. If this is the case, then skip this item and focus and the Motivation Text.
    • Motivation Text: A brief text (2000 characters max.) explaining how engaging with the current theme "Connections", participating in the program and learning about creative digital tools will nurture their current art practice.
    • Contribution: Participants are asked to submit a brief outline describing a contribution they are willing to bring to the space. This can be in the form of a workshop, a short talk or an activity such as a warmup practice.
  • Fuzzy Binaires will then tailor the workshops based on the general interest of the applicants.
  • Applicants should bring their own laptops.
  • The program cannot provide accomodation.

Humans of Fuzzy Binaires

Isabel Paehr

Isabel Paehr works as a media artist, game developer and researcher in Berlin. Isabel’s research interest in both her written and material work focuses on the relation between virtuality and concepts of the nonhuman. Her modes of presentation are exhibitions, city walks, online spaces, games and publications. From 2011-17 she studied new media and digital creation at the school of Art and Design Kassel, Germany and Kyoto Seika University, Japan.

She has been part of several interdisciplinary research projects, co-organised game festivals and taught game development in the department of Software Engineering at Kassel University. Isabel is 1/2 of the experimental game studio Topicbird.

Diego Valverde

Diego Valverde is a Computer Scientist and a canvas artist from Costa Rica, currently based in Munich. Diego has more that a decade of experience working for the software industry in the fields of electronics, software development and machine learning.

He was also a teacher in the public university in Costa Rica. He is a very friendly and curious person who loves to learn and to teach.

Agustín Ramos Anzorena

Agustín Ramos Anzorena is an electronic and new media artist from Argentina. He is interested in exploring light, shape and abstraction in visual composition, as well as in creatively understanding the very substrate of computer programming. His work has been exhibited in electronic art festivals in Buenos Aires, New York, France, Mexico and Brazil.

He teaches courses in creative coding and computational thinking at the Master in Technology and Aesthetics of Electronic Arts, at UNTREF University, as well as in various important cultural hubs in Buenos Aires. He loves introducing artists from diverse backgrounds to the joys of computer arts.

His residencies and artistic development programmes include: School for Poetic Computation (New York, USA), Center for Digital Arts and Experimental Media (University of Washington, Seattle, USA), The Recurse Center (New York, USA), Centre Intermondes “Espace International de Résidence Artistique” within Festival Zero1 (La Rochelle, France), the Understanding Visual Music International Conference for FullDome Projections. He was also selected for the 2018/2019 INNOVART French/Argentinian Programme for artistic and scientific innovation research grant.
